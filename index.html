Before the age of men, the world was filled with Elves.

Before the age of elves, the world was filled with magic.

Before the age of magic, the world was filled with song.

Before the age of song, the world was just a Word.

And before the Word there was only thought and light.

But before all the ages of men and elves, and before the ages of magic and song, and before thought and light combined to make the Word, there was shadow.


But the shadow and the evil it contained were forgotten.

Not it will wait no longer.


But there is one person who remembers. Older than time and light itself, he was there at the beginning. And though his mind is muddled, as shadow of a memory still remains. But will it be enough?

Can he stop the wars of elves and men long enough to fight this infinite evil?

Or will the world be forced to remember an evil it took millenia to forget?

ignis fatuus (n.)

"will o' the wisp, jack-o-lantern," 1560s, Medieval Latin, literally "foolish fire;" see igneous + fatuous. "It seems to have been formerly a common phenomenon; but is now exceedingly rare" [OED].

see ignis fatuus, on etymonline

Vliecke (n.)

"arrow-maker," early 14c. (as a surname attested from 1203), from Old French flechier "maker of arrows," from fleche "arrow," which is probably from Frankish, from Proto-Germanic *fleug-ika- (compare Old Low German fliuca, Middle Dutch vliecke), from PIE *pluk- "to fly," extended form of root *pleu- "to flow."

see Vliecke, on etymonline
